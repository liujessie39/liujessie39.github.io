Extracurriculars

Outside of academics, I enjoy a variety of hobbies and extracurricular activities. I spent some time in undergrad as a part of the Georgia Tech RoboJackets on their RoboCup and Outreach teams, mentoring high school robotics teams and creating training materials for K-12 robotics programs. I've been an active part of the Georgia Tech Chorale, with some hiatuses during internships and COVID-19, since Fall 2017. I've sung as a part of church choirs and even once with the Atlanta Symphony Orchestra when the Georgia Tech choirs were invited for a few Polar Express in Concert performances. Music for me, as a vocalist and pianist, is something I enjoy very much and hope to continue pursuing even after graduation. On my own time, I enjoy cooking, embroidery, and video games.

BEST (Boosting Engineering, Science, and Technology) Robotics Georgia Hub Planning Committee
  •   Planning Committee Member May 2017- May 2018

  •   Co-Director May 2018 - May 2022

  •   Hub Coordinator May 2022 - Present

The extracurricular I've been most involved with is Georgia BEST Robotics, a middle and high school robotics competition. I competed as a high schooler and joined the planning committee upon graduation. The Georgia BEST Hub is unique amongst BEST Inc. in that it's run primarily by alumni, whereas other hubs are run mostly by universities and other similar organizations. I first began as a planning committee member, becoming Co-Director in 2018, but stepping down to Hub Coordinator in 2022 in order to better focus on my graduate studies.

As a part of the planning committee, I've acted as event manager and volunteer coordinator since 2018, managing as many as 20 schools and 60 volunteers each season. We coordinate events, competitions, training, kit and material distribution, and volunteer hospitality. During my time as Co-Director, I managed communications and registration with all the schools and teachers as well. This organization is very important to me because it provides a high quality and free STEM experience for students across the state. BEST provides all building materials to students each year and no participation fees are collected from schools. This experience has taught me a lot about project management, leadership, and communication. Seeing the students having fun during our events and watching their robots succeed is an absolute highlight of my year.

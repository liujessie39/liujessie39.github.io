My Favorite Projects

Exploring PLM & the Digital Thread

During my internship at Steelcase, I got to explore the digital thread - a concept within PLM (product lifecycle management) where technology, PLM softwares, and metadata are leveraged to facilitate concurrent work, collaboration, and faster time to market. I had the amazing opportunity to present the findings of my exploration to people and leadership from numerous different teams all across Steelcase.

1st Place SICPEA Competition

SICPEA (the Student Innovation Competition - Promoting Equity & Access) is a competition that the College of Engineering (CoE) at Georgia Tech hosts to allow students to propose solutions to inequitable areas within the CoE. My team and I won first place with our proposals to improve equity for women in an undergraduate design course and makerspace at Georgia Tech.

  • More

Redesigning a Table Lock Housing

At Steelcase, I was tasked with redesiging a lock housing critical to the design of a table from its original die-cast zinc-aluminum alloy to be made with injection molded glass-filled nylon. This project was not only complex in the engineering required, but it also involved cost analyses and creating business cases, giving me an overall wholistic experience into what it takes to take a project from idea to production.

  • More

Collaborative Design & Pill Dispensers

As a part of my industrial design minor's capston course, my teammate and I conducted several collaborative interviews and design sessions to find a problem and prototype a solution for someone with mild cognitive impairement (MCI). It was a great experience to mesh together a variety of different skillsets I'd learned throughout the years and put them to the test.
